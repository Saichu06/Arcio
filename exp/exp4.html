Aim
To interface a Light Dependent Resistor (LDR) with the ESP32 and automatically control an LED based on ambient light intensity.
Objective
Read analog ambient-light values from an LDR sensor through the ESP32's ADC channel and use the reading to automatically switch an LED ON in low-light (dark) conditions and OFF in bright conditions — simulating an automatic street-light / night-lamp system.
Components Used
  • ESP32 (Dual-core Tensilica, WiFi-enabled, ADC input)
  • LDR — Light Dependent Resistor (Photoresistor, Analog Input)
  • LED (5mm, any color, 20mA forward current)
  • 10kΩ pull-down resistor (voltage divider for LDR)
  • 220Ω resistor (current limiting for LED)
Procedure
  • Place an ESP32 on the canvas from the Component Library
  • Place an LDR sensor and connect its output to ESP32 pin A0 (ADC)
  • Place an LED and connect ESP32 GPIO 2 to the LED input
  • Click Analyze Circuit to validate that the sensor and output are correctly wired
  • Click Run Simulation, then drag the LDR's light-level slider to simulate changing ambient light and observe the LED respond automatically
Expected Output
As the simulated ambient light level drops below the threshold (50%), the ESP32 reads a high analog value on A0, interprets the condition as "dark", and drives GPIO 2 HIGH — turning the LED ON. As the light level rises above the threshold, the analog reading drops, the condition becomes "bright", and the LED turns OFF automatically.
Learning Outcome
Students understand how analog sensors are read via a microcontroller's ADC, how analog values are mapped to digital decisions using a threshold, and how this forms the basis of real-world automatic lighting and IoT sensing systems.
